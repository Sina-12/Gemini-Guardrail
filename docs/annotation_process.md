The annotation process, in theory, should have gone pretty straightforwardly once we had our data. All we had to do was read the arguments, read their summaries, and assign 3 simple values to each. However, as we were behind in actually assembling our corpus; had to do extensive preprocessing for our data (i.e. feed each argument in the same format through a LLM to obtain their summaries), the process was much more frantic and time-consuming than anticipated.

As a result, we did not have much data that was annotated by more than one person, but we did all choose a section of data to annotate for more robust interannotator agreement scoring. We each uploaded our own annotations of the sections we were assigned (found under data/0-43_Agreement.csv, data/44-86_Agreement.csv, data/87-129_Agreement.csv, and data/Scraped_Agreement.csv. For the chunk that we all annotated, we sent our annotations to Jai, who uploaded it all into one file. No code was used for annotation, as it all had to be done manually.
